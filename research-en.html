How the tool supports allocation decisions

Method

How the final signal is produced

1

Read cross-market indicators

Track equity volatility, rate volatility, index drawdowns, sentiment, credit spreads, dollar liquidity, and financial conditions.

2

Separate opportunity from risk

Fear and drawdowns may create opportunity; deteriorating credit and liquidity determine when the risk budget should contract.

3

Apply credit and volatility gates

Add only when opportunity is high and risk remains controlled; reduce when credit stress and high volatility appear together.

4

Output an allocation action

The final action is limited to ADD, ADD SMALL, HOLD, or REDUCE so investors can move directly into an execution discussion.

Opportunity Lens

When to consider adding

A market decline alone is not an add signal. The setup requires a meaningful price discount, fear or volatility washout, and no systemic deterioration in credit. Historical evidence favors adding risk in stages under those conditions.

  • SPY or QQQ has a meaningful drawdown and clear price discount.
  • Fear & Greed, AAII, or Put/Call indicates rising fear.
  • HY/IG OAS, NFCI, and bank relative performance are not deteriorating together.

Risk Lens

When to consider reducing

A reduce signal does not attempt to call the market top. It identifies an environment in which the portfolio risk budget should contract. When credit stress, liquidity pressure, and high volatility appear together, reduce vulnerable exposures first.

  • Credit spreads widen, HYG/JNK decline, and NFCI or DXY indicates rising pressure.
  • Realized volatility and VIX rise together, forcing a smaller risk budget.
  • When market clearing remains incomplete, reducing takes priority over bottom fishing.

Action Rules

Execution framework for ADD, HOLD, and REDUCE

ADD

High opportunity, controlled risk, a deep SPY drawdown, and limited credit stress support adding equity exposure in planned tranches.

ADD_SMALL

Opportunity is emerging but not fully mature. Modestly accelerate contributions or rebalancing rather than taking a full-size tactical position.

HOLD

There is neither a high-conviction add window nor a systemic risk signal. Maintain allocation discipline and wait for a clearer edge.

REDUCE

Credit deterioration and high volatility appear together. Reduce or hedge part of the risk budget, starting with high-beta, leveraged, and illiquid positions.
